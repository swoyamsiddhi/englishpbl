Why Tagore's Short Stories Matter

Rabindranath Tagore, the first non-European Nobel laureate in Literature (1913), was not only a poet and philosopher but a master of the short story form. His Bengali tales, written between 1891 and 1895, remain among the most psychologically acute and socially aware works of fiction ever produced.

These thirteen stories span themes of caste, gender, colonialism, childhood, desire, and institutional power — each told with Tagore's signature blend of lyricism and devastating insight.
